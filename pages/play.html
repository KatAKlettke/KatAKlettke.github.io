Play: Worldbuilding

Dark Ages // ShizophrenAI // Traveling Magic // Yvari

I like to worldbuild high concept worlds. Like, what if there were no beasts of burden? How would that change society? (It was no fun, I scratched that and made Yvari a normal fantasy world.)

What if space and time were malleable and fluid outside of places that are fixed? What if your state of mind affected your travel time and ease? Traveling Magic is my attempt to imagine this. I'm not sure it's a good one, but I enjoyed it nevertheless.

What if AIs could have mental health issues as well? I would like to explore that question in a utopian world where humanity is utterly depending on a benevolent AI that suddenly develops shizophrenia. So far, ShizophrenAi is on the backburner because I want to treat the subject with respect and that requires more research than I could invest time in right now.

Finally, like many, I'm fascinated by the "old gods" of Europe. Combine that with Charlemagne and American Gods, two more special interests of mine, and Europe's Dark Ages from the 4th to the 7th century becomes a battlefield of the supernatural. The Christian God versus the Norse Pantheon, with the Christian God winning in the end, culminating in Charlemagne's coronation on christmas day 800 in Aachen

Play: Writing

Originally, my worldbuilding project Yvari started as a writing project. Worldbuilding for the sake of itself proved too much fun, and so the stories have taken the backseat for now.

Still, I have stories to tell, if not the skills to do so yet, so thinking about writing is always happening in some corner of my mind. We'll see if and when I will actually start writing again.

Writing as a craft is still something I'm interested in, and I regularly read books on the craft. In the future, I will gather my thoughts on some of the books I read and possibly post here.

Play: Apps to practice tech

WWWD // Expense Tracking App

When Will We Deliver is a small app that helps scrum masters with planning. Using statistical calculations, it asks for the last seven sprint velocities, sprint length, planned story points and then offers you a date by which you most likely will be done.

With my Expense Tracking App I want to tackle the problem of account statements often being very unintuitive to understand. There's a lot of fintech apps out there trying to help you, but a) I don't want my sensitive data in the hands of people who have an incentive to sell it, and b) hey, why not try to create one for my own use, offline?
